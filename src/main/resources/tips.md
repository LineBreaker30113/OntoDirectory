Use Onto Directory as your primary long-term archive. It is engineered to safely hold tens of thousands of files without folder friction.
---
You can import entire directories at once. Onto Directory will automatically convert your old folder structure into Ontology Tags.
---
Right-click any file in the Explorer to quickly rename its actual filename or extract a copy of it to your operating system.
---
To quickly mass-tag files, highlight them in the File Explorer, then drag and drop them directly onto a Tag in the Hierarchy Tree.
---
You can build "Classification Perspectives." Try creating a root tag called 'ByExtension' with children like 'PDF' and 'PNG', and assign them to your files!
---
The Left Sidebar can be collapsed using the top arrow icon to give you a distraction-free view of your workspace.
---
If you make a mistake—like accidentally deleting a massive tag—don't panic. Just press Ctrl+Z to undo your last action. The graph will instantly repair itself.
---
Double-click any Tag in the Hierarchy Tree to instantly filter the File Explorer to that specific Domain.
---
If your Data Lake starts to feel sluggish after months of deleting and organizing, click the 'Optimize' button to safely defragment the database.
---
You can chain multiple AND, OR, and SUBTRACT operators in the Filter Builder to isolate highly specific files in seconds.
---
Want to attach a physical note to a cluster of files? Create a parent Tag for them, and attach the note to the Tag. (Notes Module coming soon!)
---
Hover your mouse over any file in the Explorer for a second to reveal a clean tooltip listing every Tag assigned to it.
---
Hold CTRL while clicking in the Hierarchy Tree to select multiple Tags at once, allowing for bulk deletion or mass assignment.
